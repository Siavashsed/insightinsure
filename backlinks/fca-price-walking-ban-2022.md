## QUORA
**Answer this question:** Did the FCA price walking ban actually make home and car insurance cheaper in the UK?

The FCA's price walking ban, which came into force in January 2022, didn't make insurance cheaper in the way many people hoped. But it did change something important about how insurers operate.

Before the ban, insurance companies had a profitable strategy: charge loyal customers significantly more at renewal than they'd charge identical new customers. It was perfectly legal and incredibly common. The FCA finally prohibited this practice, meaning insurers had to charge the same renewal rate to existing customers as they would to new ones.

However—and this is the critical part—the ban didn't prevent overall price increases. Instead, insurers adjusted their baseline pricing. If they could no longer charge loyal customers more, they raised the standard rate for everyone. The result was that renewal quotes remained high or increased, but now they applied fairly across the board.
