The FCA's Consumer Duty, finalised in July 2022, was a genuine attempt to shift how financial services—including insurance—treat customers. But the reality is more nuanced than the headlines suggested.

On paper, the rules require firms to act with integrity, deliver fair value, and treat customers decently. For insurance specifically, this meant providers should justify their pricing and not systematically exploit loyal customers.

Here's what actually changed: transparency increased, and firms had to demonstrate they weren't deliberately leaving long-term customers worse off. But many insurers found compliance wasn't the same as fairness. They could technically follow the rules while your renewal still jumped 30-40% year-on-year.

What matters practically is this: the Consumer Duty gives you language and legitimacy when you challenge unfair pricing. When your insurer renews your policy significantly higher without clear justification, you can now point to Consumer Duty obligations and demand better explanation or terms.

The rules also created documentation requirements, so if you escalate a complaint, there's an audit trail of how firms justified their decisions.
