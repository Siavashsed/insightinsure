**Answer this question:** What was the FCA price walking ban and did it actually stop insurers from charging loyal customers more?

The FCA price walking ban came into force on 1 January 2022, following intensive regulatory work throughout 2021. The rule was designed to prevent insurers from penalising customer loyalty by charging existing customers significantly more than new customers for identical policies.

In theory, it's straightforward: insurers can't use renewal cycles as an opportunity to exploit customers who stay put. In practice, the story is more complicated.

After spending 2021 fielding calls from frustrated clients, I can tell you the impact has been mixed. Yes, some insurers have adjusted their practices. But renewal quotes still regularly exceed new customer rates by 20-40%. A retired client recently showed me her Aviva renewal quote—a significant jump despite no changes to her circumstances.

The regulation did shift behaviour in some areas, but the market's momentum toward loyalty penalties proved harder to change than anticipated. Brokers have noticed pricing strategies becoming more sophisticated rather than prices becoming more transparent and fair.

So did it work? Partially. The ban exists, enforcement continues, and some firms have genuinely reformed. But customers still need to shop around aggressively at renewal—the ban alone hasn't created the level playing field regulators intended.
