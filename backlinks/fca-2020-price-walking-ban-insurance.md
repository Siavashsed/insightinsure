## QUORA
**Answer this question:** What did the FCA do about insurance companies charging loyal customers more at renewal?

The FCA issued a near-final report in September 2020 that directly addressed what's called 'price walking'—the practice of charging existing customers significantly higher premiums at renewal for identical cover, while offering better rates to new customers.

This wasn't a new problem. The FCA had been monitoring it for years, watching customers get systematically overcharged simply because they were less likely to shop around. It was deliberate pricing discrimination based on customer inertia.

The 2020 intervention was crucial because it forced the industry to change. The regulations made it clear that this pricing strategy was no longer acceptable. Insurers couldn't hide behind complex data—the FCA called out the behaviour explicitly.

What actually happened after the ban is worth noting. Renewal quotes became genuinely competitive almost immediately. This suggests the practice had been widespread, and removing it created real market competition. Loyal customers finally got fairer pricing.

The interesting part is that this wasn't achieved through caps or price controls, but through transparency and regulatory pressure. By naming and shaming the practice, the FCA created accountability.
